[Stub: introduce the Alps as a continental-scale climate divide separating maritime Western Europe from the Po Valley and Adriatic. Föhn winds, lake-effect precipitation in the foreland basins, and the rain shadow that defines northern Italy's hot summers all depend on the range existing.]

[Stub: describe the edit. The "after" heightmap takes the Alpine arc — roughly from the Maritime Alps to the Eastern Alps — down to a mean elevation of ~400 m, with edges feathered into the surrounding lowlands so Orogen doesn't see a step function. This is a counterfactual, not a tectonic forecast.]

[Stub: walk through what the model says. Expect the Köppen Cfb (oceanic) climate of southern Germany to extend further south. Expect Csa (hot-summer Mediterranean) to retreat as moister Atlantic air can now penetrate the Po Valley. Temperature in the formerly Alpine region rises by several degrees from the lapse rate alone.]

[Stub: caveats. Orogen's wind module simulates large-scale flow but does not resolve foehn-style local descent or convective triggering. So "no Alps" in the model misses a lot of the *mechanism* by which the Alps shape European weather, even where it gets the broad climate-type response right.]
