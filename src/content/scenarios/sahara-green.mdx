[Stub: explain that for several millennia in the early-to-mid Holocene, the Sahara was not desert. Lakes covered parts of what is now Mauritania, Mali, and Chad; gallery forest extended hundreds of kilometers north of where it sits today. Cite deMenocal 2000 for the abrupt-termination evidence in marine cores off West Africa.]

[Stub: explain the orbital forcing. Earth's precession cycle put Northern Hemisphere summer perihelion roughly opposite to where it sits now, strengthening the West African monsoon enough to push the rainfall belt deep into the modern desert. The strengthened monsoon, combined with vegetation–albedo feedback, produced a stable wet state.]

[Stub: walk through the maps. The Köppen view shows the modern desert (BWh) replaced by tropical savanna (Aw) and steppe (BSh). Note the visible northward shift of the ITCZ in the temperature panel.]

[Stub: caveats. Orogen runs a heuristic climate model on a 2:1 elevation grid; it does not couple vegetation, sea-surface temperature anomalies, or orbital changes directly. The "after" PNG was produced by tweaking precipitation multipliers in a band across North Africa to mimic the AHP rainfall climatology — not by physically forcing the model. Treat this as illustration, not reconstruction.]
